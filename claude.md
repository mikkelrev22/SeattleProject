#Project description
- I want to create a responsive React website that uses a progressive disclosure approach to personalize the user journey with AI. The landing page will have a contextual feed of cards and below that will be a prompt for the user to select an answer from clickable choices. The cards will be clickable and show information like “price of ticket for certain game on stub hub” or “top 3 food trucks near the stadium" and “rainy day activities in Seattle.” If they click on a card, the other cards will be updated to reflect what they selected. For example, if they clicked on the food trucks card, they would be provided with the answer and maybe an external link and the other cards would update to provide information related to their choice. The prompts and questions will be generated by an AI agent or ambient AI which will be given a certain set of instructions / prompts to guide it through what kind of questions to ask. The questions could be something like, “What team do you like?”, “where are you staying?”, and “What game are you attending?”, “What interests you?” and “Are you traveling alone or with others?” Every time the user makes a selection, the cards and the prompt will update with data that reflects the user’s preferences and context. After 2 selections and updates, the user will be prompted to create an account and persist their data. This way, when they revisit the site, they can use the stored context and continue with their personalized experience. The premise of the site is to provide information on Seattle for fans coming for the 2026 World Cup. I assume that fans will want to know information on events, food, accommodations, ticket information, weather, transportation, and more. This information can be sourced from the web, but also through a RAG search on a vector database from a trusted knowledge base. The landing page can also include a countdown banner for when the world cup will start and possibly fun stats and a progress bar for what was learned about the user so far through their answers. There can also be metrics for the AI such as an upvote and downvote on the refined results.
#Style description
- The style of the website should bold, yet Seattle-themed
#Live Claude AI
— every card refresh and every question is a real Claude API call. The AI reads your selections and adapts both the question and all 4 cards dynamically.
#Progressive disclosure flow 
— answer a question, and the AI simultaneously generates a new question AND refreshes the cards to match your profile. After 2 selections, the signup modal fires automatically.
#Card system 
— cards are clickable to expand with full details, external links, and up/down vote feedback. The AI tracks your votes conceptually for future personalization.
#Countdown + profile progress 
— the live countdown to June 11, 2026 is ticking, and the profile bar + topic pills fill in as the AI learns about you (team, dates, food preferences, etc.).
#Signup modal 
— shows a summary of what the AI has learned, collects email + name, and persists the session greeting.
#Architecture decisions
- architecture to build toward is React + Next.js for the full app, with a Supabase backend for user persistence and a vector DB (like Pinecone or pgvector) for the RAG knowledge base.
